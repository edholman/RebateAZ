Tempe, AZ Commission Rebate for Home Buyers Near ASU and the Urban Core

Updated February 2026 • 6 min read

Tempe, Arizona is one of the most in-demand markets in the Valley, and it's not hard to see why. ASU's main campus anchors the city, but the buyer pool here goes well beyond students and faculty, major tech, biotech, and semiconductor employers along the I-10 and ASU Research Park corridor bring in a steady stream of relocating professionals. Whether you're buying a house in South Tempe, a condo near the university, or a townhome close to Tempe Town Lake's waterfront, a buyer commission rebate can help reduce closing costs and put real money back in your pocket at the table.

Tempe homes typically run $400,000 to $700,000, which puts a 2% rebate in the $8,000 to $14,000 range. After closing, there's no shortage of ways to spend locally, Mill Avenue's restaurant and brewery scene is one of the Valley's best, and Tempe Town Lake's trails and kayak rentals are right there for weekend afternoons. Tempe’s Housing Market at a Glance

Tempe is a dense, land-constrained city which means inventory is tight and prices hold well. New construction is nearly nonexistent here — most buyers are competing on resale. The product mix is the most varied in the East Valley: single-family homes in South Tempe, condos and townhomes near the university and Town Lake, and small-lot homes in established neighborhoods that have been renovating steadily. Tech and semiconductor employers at the ASU Research Park (Amazon, State Farm, LifeLock, and others) create year-round relocation buyer demand that keeps the market competitive.

Popular Tempe Neighborhoods

  • South Tempe / Kyrene corridor: The most family-friendly part of Tempe, with larger lots, established trees, top-rated schools, and prices in the $550,000–$800,000 range. Warner Ranch and The Lakes are among the most desirable communities here.
  • Tempe Town Lake: High-rise condos, townhomes, and new construction along the waterfront. Attracts professionals who want walkability and a urban feel, $500,000–$1.2M.
  • Downtown Tempe / Mill Avenue: Condos and older SFH close to dining, music venues, and the light rail. Mix of owner-occupied and investor units, $380,000–$650,000.
  • University area: Primarily condos, student-adjacent but with a growing owner-occupant base. Buyer rebates work well here since the numbers are tight, $300,000–$500,000.
  • Rural Road corridor: Established neighborhoods between Rural and McClintock, family-oriented, walkable to schools, $480,000–$700,000.

Schools in Tempe

South Tempe is largely served by Kyrene Elementary School District, which is one of the most consistently high-rated districts in the state and a major reason home values in south Tempe stay elevated. Central and north Tempe falls under Tempe Elementary School District. High school students in most of Tempe attend Tempe Union High School District schools, including Marcos de Niza, Tempe High, and Corona del Sol (south Tempe). Verifying school assignment by address is important given the district boundaries across the city.

Condos and HOA Considerations

Tempe has one of the highest concentrations of condo and townhome inventory in the Valley. For condo purchases, lender guidelines around HOA financial health, owner-occupancy ratios, and building reserves can affect how the rebate is applied. Cash buyers have more flexibility. Confirm with your lender early in the process how closing credits will be structured on a condo transaction.

How the Rebate Is Applied

  • Reduces the cash you bring to closing
  • Offsets prepaid expenses like homeowner’s insurance and tax escrow
  • Can cover title insurance or loan origination fees
  • May assist with an interest rate buydown (confirm with your lender)
