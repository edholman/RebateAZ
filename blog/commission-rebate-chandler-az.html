Home Buyer Commission Rebates in Chandler, Arizona

Updated February 2026 • 7 min read

Chandler, Arizona is one of the East Valley’s most desirable addresses — home to the Intel campus, a thriving tech corridor along the 101, and some of the best-rated schools in the state. With median home prices in the $500,000–$650,000 range, a buyer commission rebate can translate into real money back in your pocket at closing.

How Rebates Work in Chandler

In most Chandler transactions, the seller offers compensation to the buyer’s agent as part of the listing agreement. When structured properly through RebateAZ, a portion of that compensation is returned to you as a credit on your closing disclosure. The rebate doesn’t change the purchase price — it directly reduces your cash needed at closing.

Chandler’s Housing Market at a Glance

Chandler’s real estate market is driven by strong employer demand. The Intel semiconductor campus, major financial services firms along Price Road, and easy Loop 101/202 freeway access make Chandler a top pick for buyers relocating from California and the Pacific Northwest. Competition can be brisk, especially in the $450K–$600K range — which is exactly where a $9,000–$12,000 rebate credit makes the biggest difference.

School Districts in Chandler

Chandler is served by two highly-rated public school districts: Chandler Unified School District (CUSD) and portions of Kyrene School District in the northwest. CUSD consistently ranks among Arizona’s top districts, which is a major draw for families and supports strong resale demand across all Chandler ZIP codes.

New Construction in Chandler

While most of Chandler is built out, infill new construction still appears near downtown and along the Chandler/Gilbert border. If you’re considering a new build, your agent must be registered with the builder on the first visit — builder policies vary and registration timing can affect eligibility for buyer agent compensation.

How the Rebate Is Applied

Rebates appear as a credit on your settlement statement (closing disclosure). Depending on your lender’s guidelines, the credit can:

  • Reduce cash to close
  • Offset prepaid items (insurance, property tax escrow)
  • Cover loan origination fees or title costs
  • Potentially assist with a rate buydown (ask your lender)

Financing vs. Cash Purchases

If you’re financing, your lender must approve the rebate structure — most conventional, FHA, and VA loans allow buyer credits up to certain limits. Cash buyers have more flexibility in how credits are structured, though all rebates still must be documented through escrow regardless of loan type.
