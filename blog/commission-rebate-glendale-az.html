Get a Commission Rebate on Your Glendale, Arizona Home Purchase

Updated February 2026 • 6 min read

Glendale, Arizona doesn't get the same press as Scottsdale or Chandler, but it's one of the most accessible and underrated homebuying markets in the Phoenix metro. The city anchors the West Valley's sports and entertainment scene, State Farm Stadium (home of the Arizona Cardinals) and the Glendale sports arena sit right in the middle of it, and the Westgate entertainment district has become a legitimate dining and nightlife destination. Meanwhile, the Arrowhead area in north Glendale offers established single-family homes with Peoria-adjacent pricing and a quieter suburban feel.

Homes in Glendale typically range from $350,000 for smaller resale properties to $600,000+ in nicer north Glendale neighborhoods. At those price points, a 2% buyer rebate puts $7,000 to $12,000 in your pocket. After closing, it's easy to spend that locally, Catlin Court in Old Town Glendale has one of the Valley's best antique shopping scenes, and the restaurant scene around Westgate keeps growing. If you're weighing Glendale against Peoria or Avondale, those guides are worth reading before you decide.

Glendale’s Housing Market at a Glance

Glendale’s market breaks into two distinct zones. South and central Glendale offers older resale inventory at $320,000–$480,000, attracting first-time buyers and investors. North Glendale — particularly the Arrowhead corridor — is a more established suburban market with homes in the $450,000–$700,000 range and buyers who tend to stay put. The city benefits from major employer anchors: State Farm Stadium, the Glendale Arena, Banner Thunderbird Medical Center, and a significant loop freeway network that makes commuting across the metro manageable.

Popular Glendale Neighborhoods

  • Arrowhead Ranch: Master-planned community in north Glendale with lakes, golf, and HOA amenities. One of the most desirable zip codes in the West Valley, $480,000–$750,000.
  • North Glendale / 83rd Ave corridor: Established single-family neighborhoods with mature trees and larger lots, good schools, $420,000–$600,000.
  • Westgate area: Newer condos, townhomes, and single-family homes within walking distance of the stadium and arena complex. Convenient for buyers who want urban access without downtown Phoenix pricing, $380,000–$560,000.
  • Downtown Glendale / Catlin Court: Older historic homes with character, walkable to antique shops and local restaurants, $300,000–$450,000. Popular with buyers who want something distinct from the master-planned suburban grid.
  • Sunrise Vista / Luke AFB-adjacent: Strong demand from military buyers, solid resale history, $360,000–$500,000.

Schools in Glendale

Glendale spans multiple school districts. Central and south Glendale falls under Glendale Elementary School District and Glendale Union High School District. North Glendale is primarily served by Deer Valley Unified School District, which includes several highly rated schools and is one of the reasons north Glendale real estate holds its value well. Confirming district boundaries by address is important given how much they vary across zip codes.

How the Rebate Is Applied

In most Glendale transactions the rebate appears as a credit on your Closing Disclosure. Depending on your lender’s guidelines it can:

  • Reduce the cash you bring to closing
  • Offset prepaid expenses (homeowner’s insurance, property tax escrow)
  • Cover title insurance or origination fees
  • Potentially assist with an interest rate buydown (confirm with your lender)

For cash purchases the structure is generally more flexible but must be documented through escrow.
