Buying a Home in Buckeye? Here's How to Get a Commission Rebate

Updated February 2026 • 6 min read

Buckeye, Arizona has been one of the fastest-growing cities in the entire United States for several years running, and if you've driven out on the I-10 past Goodyear lately, you've seen why. New master-planned communities are going up constantly, from the lifestyle-forward Verrado village near the White Tank Mountains to the active-adult Sun City Festival, to new neighborhoods spreading south from the Watson Road corridor. Builders like Taylor Morrison, Pulte, and Toll Brothers all have a presence here.

With most Buckeye homes priced between $380,000 and $550,000, a 2% buyer commission rebate can put $7,600 to $11,000 back in your pocket at closing. Buying new? That rebate can go toward furnishing rooms in a blank-slate home, installing desert landscaping, or starting the pool fund early. Buckeye's growth shows no signs of slowing, and getting in now, with a rebate that offsets your closing costs, is one of the smarter moves in the West Valley right now.

Buckeye’s Housing Market at a Glance

Buckeye stretches across a large geographic footprint west of Goodyear, and the market reflects that range. You’ll find value-priced resale homes in the low $400,000s near older neighborhoods along Buckeye Road, and luxury new construction pushing $800,000+ in Verrado’s higher-end phases. Most buyers land between $400,000 and $580,000. The city’s rapid growth has attracted major retail, dining, and medical infrastructure, so the "you have to drive far for everything" concern that existed five years ago is largely gone.

Popular Buckeye Communities

  • Verrado: One of the most successful master-planned communities in Arizona, with a walkable Main Street, parks, pools, and White Tank Mountain views. Homes range from $450,000 to $800,000+, with distinct phases at different price points.
  • Sun City Festival: Active-adult (55+) community with golf, resort amenities, and a strong resale market. Typically $350,000–$550,000 depending on model and lot.
  • Tartesso: Value-focused master-planned community on the far west side, popular with first-time buyers and families, $380,000–$490,000.
  • Watson Ranch area: Growing corridor south of I-10, mix of builders and price points, newer builds with larger lot sizes, $420,000–$560,000.
  • Sundance: Established resale community with HOA amenities, more mature landscaping than newer developments, $400,000–$530,000.

Schools in Buckeye

Buckeye is served by Buckeye Elementary School District (K–8) and Buckeye Union High School District. Verrado residents are typically zoned into Litchfield Elementary School District and Verrado High School, which consistently earns strong ratings. School boundaries vary significantly by neighborhood, so confirming assignment before you make an offer is important — especially in newer developments where boundaries can shift.

New Construction in Buckeye

Buckeye has some of the highest new construction volume in the entire Phoenix metro. Active builders include Taylor Morrison, Pulte, Toll Brothers, D.R. Horton, and Ashton Woods, with communities spread across Verrado, Watson Ranch, and the southern 303 corridor. If you visit any builder model without your agent present, you may lose eligibility for buyer agent compensation entirely. Always register your agent on the first visit.

How the Rebate Is Applied

  • Reduces the cash you bring to closing
  • Offsets prepaid expenses like homeowner’s insurance and tax escrow
  • Can cover title insurance or loan origination fees
  • May assist with an interest rate buydown (confirm with your lender)
